PHRASES and CLAUSES

THEME AND SUMMARY OF THE STORY :

In the mythological story “How the kangaroo got her pouch”, the author explores the theme of kindness. We learn that once, the great god Byamee wanted to find the most kind creature, so he descended from the sky and disguised himself as an old, sick and uncared for wombat, who is seeking help for sustenance and care. The wombat’s interaction with the kangaroo is pictured in this story. Byamee was overjoyed to see the kangaroo’s kindness right from the beginning as she cared for the wombat like she would do for her own joey. Leading the wombat to the juiciest grass, cleanest water and selflessly saving him from the hunter won Byamee’s heart . Consequently, the great god Byamee gifted the kangaroo with her pouch so that she never loses her joey as she had lost the joey while helping the wombat. On seeing the kangaroo’s kindness for her fellow animals, Byamee also gifted pouches to all marsupials so that their babies would never get lost again.

PHRASES and CLAUSES- OVERVIEW:

Phrases and Clauses are both parts of a sentence with different features and roles. A phrase is a combination of words which cannot produce meaning independently and does not have a subject-verb combination. Clauses are groups of words which can produce meaning independently and always contain a subject-verb combination.
Phrases are not divided into types based on structure and function. Clauses are divided into Subordinate/Dependent clauses and Principal/Independent Clauses.
Principal Clauses can produce complete meaning on their own and thus are sentences. Subordinate clauses depend on Principal clauses to make complete sense. Subordinate Clauses are introduced in sentences by subordinate conjunctions and Relative Pronouns.
Principal Clause do not have types but Subordinate clauses have quite some types which will discussed later in detail.
